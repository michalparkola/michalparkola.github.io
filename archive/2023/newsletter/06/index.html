In the previous issue I shared a Stoic suggestion that to be happy we need to focus more on things we can fully control (our virtues) rather than external conditions.

Level 0 satisfying wants = make sure we get what we want in the world (love, money, six pack abs, …)

Level 1 cultivating virtue = be satisfied with our internal virtues and how well we respond to things.

But maybe there’s something even better?

How about Level 2 unconditional happiness?

What if we could cultivate a sense of satisfaction regardless of the level of our external success AND the belief that we posses a certain set of positive traits?

Tell me what you think?

Liberating Structures are a set of pretty general, yet very practical patterns of organization at work.

Very useful for managers, coaches and others who help groups of people work together well.

My favorites:

  • Wicked questions
  • User-experience fishbowl
  • Purpose to practice

The Core Protocols are another set of micro-patterns for use in all kinds of meetings. A facilitators basic toolbox.

Most useful in my experience:

  • Check In
  • Pass (Unpass)
  • Decider

And while we’re on the subject of meetings: check out Lean Coffee. The absolute best meeting format since Marco Polo visited China.
